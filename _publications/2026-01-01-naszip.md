NasZip is an NDP-accelerated approximate nearest neighbor search framework for retrieval-augmented generation systems.

This work introduces a statistical-PCA-based feature-level early-exit mechanism and dynamic floating-point compression to reduce memory traffic without sacrificing recall. It also incorporates data-aware neighbor mapping and a local caching mechanism to reduce cross-channel communication overhead and improve parallelism.

The project was accepted to ISCA 2026, and I contributed as a co-first author.
